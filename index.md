# Bio431: Computational Image Analysis of Cells and Tissues

Biological image analysis is undergoing a computational revolution driven by advances in microscopy, machine learning, and the availability of large-scale datasets. This course will explore current computational methods for quantifying and analyzing microscopy images and movies of cells and tissues, illustrating how these approaches lead to biological discoveries. Students will use open-source tools to analyze publicly available and in-house microscopy datasets. Hands-on image analysis will be coupled with readings to build familiarity with the field through a combination of review articles, methodological papers, and primary literature. Topics may include automated cell segmentation and tracking, spatial organization of tissues, and cell migration during development and disease.

## Who this class is for

This class is for students who want to better understand how biological images are processed and analyzed. As part of this class, students will learn about computational concepts that have greatly influenced image analysis. Bio431 is a 0.5-unit upper-level biology course, and it is intended for students at junior or senior standing who have taken multiple biology courses. Students with an interest in image analysis from outside the sciences are also welcome to register after checking with me.

## Course goals

At the end of the semester, you will be able to:
1. Understand the state of computational image analysis tools for cellular biology. 
2. Identify and summarize the methodological steps from a scientific paper.
3. Participate in a discussion around a specific scientific finding or set of findings.
4. Communicate technical details in written and oral form.
5. Use image analysis tools to process images and interpret the outputs.
