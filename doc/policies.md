# Course Policies

There are four policies for this course: one for [attendance and engagement](#attendance-policy), one for [deadlines](#deadline-policy), one for [collaboration](#collaboration-policy), and one for the use of [generative AI](#generative-ai-policy).

## Attendance and Engagement Policy

One fo the most valuable aspects of Bio431 is the in-person content. This course meets once a week, and the meeting times will be a mix of lectures, presentations, discussions, and group work time. Students will gain a deeper and more complex understanding of the material if they engage with the material during class. **You should attend and fully participate in the scheduled meeting times.** 

However, conflicts will almost certainly crop-up. There may be cases where you cannot attend class for stretches at a time (due to illness, for example). In rare cases, there may be circumstances beyond your control where you are unable to meet the expectations outlined in this syllabus. If there are extenuating circumstances that might impact your academic performance in this class, please inform me as soon as you can. Additionally, you may want to reach out to the Student Life Office at student-life@reed.edu, phone: (503) 517-7396, office: Eliot 218. The Student Life Office is dedicated to assisting students in accessing campus and support resources, especially during challenging situations.

If you miss multiple classes, I may reach out to make sure you are OK. Communication is key.

If you must miss a class, follow these instructions to make up the in-class work:
1. **Email me before the class meets** to let me know you cannot attend. If there is planned group work that day, email the rest of your group members to notify them of your absence.
2. **Before the next class period,** email me a makeup assignment. The makeup assignment will be clearly denoted on Moodle for each week. You will be expected to actively engage in the next class period, so make sure you are prepared for that as well. 

Failing to do the instructions above will result in an incomplete for that class. Each incomplete will drop your grade by one-half a grade point (e.g., a B+ will become a B). 

### The Main Takeaway: attending and participating is expected and will set you up for success. In the rare case that you must miss a class, email me beforehand and complete the makeup assignment before the next class.

## Deadline Policy

There are deadlines for weekly work (which includes reading papers, preparing for group work, and completing small software tasks) and for longer multi-part assignments (such as the writing assignment). The purpose of these deadlines is for you to complete the assignments at a steady pace. Some of the assignments are low-stakes in that they are completed or not. Other assignments will have opportunities for resubmission if they are not complete by the deadline.  **You must submit all assignments by the posted deadlines, even if they are incomplete.**  My feedback will help you in your resubmission of the work.

If you are feeling overwhelmed, come talk to me to strategize a plan for successfully completing the common assignments.

### The Main Takeaway: Assignment deadlines are intentional, and you should submit your work by the deadline. You will be able to resubmit some assignments, and my feedback about your progress will improve your resubmissions.

## Collaboration Policy

Computational biology is an inherently collaborative field, requiring skills and perspectives from different disciplines. Bio431 will be a learning community, and so working with others is encouraged (and sometimes explicitly required). 

Assignments will be clearly denoted as _individual_ or _group_ work. All individual work must be written by you(including writing, coding, etc.). However, you may work alongside anyone for any assignment. **If you work with someone else for software tasks or writing tasks, you must acknowledge that collaboration.** There will be instructions on each assignment about how to acknowledge others.

**Written reflections are the one place where collaboration is not allowed**. The goal of written reflections is for you to get an understanding of _your_ thoughts, _your_ opinions, _your_ questions. Thus, they are the only type of assignment that must not be done in consultation with anyone else. 

Failure to properly acknoewledge collaboration and others' ideas in your assignments, or collaborating on the written reflections, are in violation fo the Honor Principle.

### The Main Takeaway: Collaboration in class is expected and encouraged, as long as you acknowledge the collaboration in assignments. Written reflections should be completed individually.

## Generative AI Policy

Generative AI is technology that is trained to generate text, images, or code from natural language prompts.  Two examples of generative AI that you might have seen before are ChatGPT (which returns text based on prompts) and GitHub Copilot (which returns code based on prompts). Generative AI is becoming ubiquitous for some, while others are keeping away from it. **This policy is intended to be flexible with generative AI usage while ensuring that you demonstrate the learning goals.**

Generative AI expectations for each assignment will be clearly specified. They will be one of the following:
- **AI is encouraged:** For some assignments, use of generative AI may be very helpful. There will be instructions on how to acknowledge your use of AI (which will include providing your prompts).
- **Limited use of AI:** For other assignments, thinking through challenging topics before resorting to AI will be important.  There will be instructions on how to acknowledge your use of AI (which will include providing your prompts).
- **Do not use AI:** For the written reflections, you are prohibited from using generative AI for the reasons cited above in the Collaboration section. You will be asked to confirm that you did not use AI.

### The Main Takeaway: assignments will have different approved levels of generative AI use, which is intended to help you navigate when it is useful and when it prevents critical thinking.
